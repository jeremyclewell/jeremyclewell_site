Gear Check

What's in your bag?

Pack smart and use the bag-check service on race day.

Like many distance races, the Blue Cross Broad Street Run offers a gear check service for runners. This convenient service shuttles your gym or duffle bag from the starting area to the finish line.

Not only does this mean that you can wear sweats before the start of the race, but you can also bring along some dry clothes, socks, and shoes for post-race.

Things you might want to pack:

  • change of socks, shoes, or sandals
  • change of clothes
  • a hat
  • favorite sports recovery bar
  • sunglasses/sunscreen
  • wet wipes
  • tissues
  • extra safety pins for attaching the race bib
  • band aids

Things not to pack in your gym bag:*

  • money
  • cell phone
  • keys

* We realize that many runners need these items for race day. In those cases, we suggest wearing an elastic waist pouch (it's like a fanny-pack that doesn't bounce around) on race day. Also, while tying keys to shoelaces is a common practice of runners, please realize the risk you run of loosing your keys.

How to use the gear check

Look for the yellow school busses above the start line area.

Bring your own gym or duffel bag for your gear.

Mark your bag with the tag supplied on race day. Any bag that is not properly marked will not be accepted.

Place your bag on the bus that displays the number that corresponds to your race bib. This system is designed to help you locate your bag at the finish area.

At the finish, display your official race number to the volunteers and your items will be returned. Please be patient and courteous, as this is a big job for our volunteers to handle.
